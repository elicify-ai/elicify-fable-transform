# Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

**Document:** elicify-vertex Contributor License Agreement — Version 1.0 (2026)

elicify-vertex accepts external contributions under this **Contributor License Agreement
(CLA)**. Before your first pull request can be merged, you (as an individual) — or
your employer, if you contribute on company time — agree to this CLA once. After
that, you are covered for all future contributions to this repository.

This CLA follows the widely used Apache and Google CLA model: **you keep the
copyright to your contribution** and simply grant elicify.ai Pte. Ltd. a broad
license to use it. You do **not** assign or give away ownership of your work.

## Why we have a CLA

elicify-vertex is developed by **elicify.ai Pte. Ltd.** as an open-source project under
the MIT license. Long-term, the project may also ship under other distribution forms (npm packages, bundled agents, hosted variants). The CLA gives us a
clear, durable license to include your contribution in the project and in those
variants, without having to track down every contributor for permission later.

**The MIT license on the code is unchanged and irrevocable.** Anyone who
downloads elicify-vertex gets the same MIT terms forever. The CLA does not relicense
existing code; it only records the license you grant for your own contributions.

## What the CLA does, in plain terms

This summary is for convenience only; the binding terms are in the agreements
below. By agreeing, you confirm that:

1. **You keep your copyright.** You are not giving your work away — you retain
   full ownership and can use it for anything else you like.
2. **You grant a copyright license.** You let elicify.ai and everyone who
   receives the project use, modify, sublicense, and redistribute your
   contribution — including in commercial variants.
3. **You grant a patent license.** If your contribution includes patentable
   ideas, you license those patents to users of the project.
4. **You allow use without attribution.** To the extent the law allows, you agree
   the project can use your contribution without crediting you and won't assert
   "moral rights" against the project or its users (see §10 below).
5. **You have the right to contribute.** Either you wrote it yourself, or your
   employer has authorised it (or signed the Corporate CLA).

## How you agree (electronic acceptance)

1. Open a pull request as normal.
2. The CLA bot comments on your PR with a one-click link.
3. Click the link, sign in with your GitHub account, and confirm.
4. Your PR is unblocked for review.

You agree **once per GitHub account**; all future PRs from that account are
covered. Clicking to accept (or, where the bot records it, submitting a
contribution after being notified of this CLA) is an electronic signature and is
as legally effective as a handwritten signature.

## If you would rather not agree

You do not have to. The MIT license lets you fork elicify-vertex, modify it, and
distribute your version freely (subject to the [trademark notice](TRADEMARKS.md)
on the name and logo). The CLA is only required to merge changes **into the
upstream elicify-vertex repository**.

## Common questions

- **Can I still use my own contribution elsewhere?** Yes. You keep your
  copyright and may use your contribution for any purpose.
- **What if my employer owns my work?** Have your employer agree to the
  **Corporate CLA** below.
- **Will elicify-vertex go closed-source?** The MIT code is irrevocable — anyone who has
  it keeps the MIT grant forever. The CLA only gives us the *option* to also
  distribute the same code under other terms in future commercial variants,
  alongside the open-source MIT version.
- **Why a CLA and not just [DCO](https://developercertificate.org/)?** DCO suits
  projects that commit to a single license. elicify-vertex needs the option to ship
  commercial variants alongside open source, which the CLA enables.

For any other questions, email **connect@elicify.ai** or open a GitHub
discussion.
